Geography

Spills by County: 2024 vs. 2025

Converse County led Wyoming with 172 spills, slightly down from 174 in 2024. Campbell County ranked second. Both counties sit atop the Powder River Basin, Wyoming's most active oil region. Laramie County fell sharply from 69 to 33, while Carbon rose from 73 to 83.

Operators

Spills by Operator: 2024 vs. 2025

Crowheart led all Wyoming operators with 102 spills, down from 145 in 2024. Continental Resources increased from 93 to 95. EOG Resources fell from 59 to 46. Amplify Energy and Barn Owls Operating saw notable year-over-year increases.

Operator names are standardized by removing legal suffixes (LLC, LP, ENERGY, RESOURCES, OPERATING, etc.) and applying company-specific corrections, following the notebook's cleaning methodology.

Volume

Top Operators by Volume Spilled, 2025 (Gallons)

Merit Energy reported the highest volume at 303,565 gallons despite ranking seventh in spill count, reflecting a small number of large incidents. Continental ranked second at 177,000 gallons and Contango third at 134,000. Barn Owls Operating, which ranked sixth in spill count, reported nearly 64,000 gallons.

Combined oil, produced water, and other. Source: WOGCC, 2025.
Year-Over-Year

Volume Spilled by Top Operators: 2024 vs. 2025 (Gallons)

Merit Energy's volume nearly tripled year-over-year, from 103,000 to 304,000 gallons. Contango more than doubled. Crowheart Energy fell by nearly 60,000 gallons despite remaining the highest-count operator.

Top 5 operators by 2025 volume. Source: WOGCC, 2025.
Seasonality

Spills by Month, 2025

February was Wyoming's peak month with 119 spills. Winter months (January–March) accounted for a disproportionate share of incidents, likely reflecting freeze-thaw stress on pipelines and equipment. Spill frequency dropped steadily through spring and summer.
