New Mexico tracks two types of incidents: liquid spills (produced water, crude oil, condensate) and gas releases (natural gas vented or flared). Gas releases dominate the count. Liquid and gas totals are analyzed separately throughout this report.
Trend

Total Incidents Per Year, 2022–2025

New Mexico reported 38,364 incidents in 2025, down from 42,213 in 2024 and well below the 2023 peak of 54,622. The 2023 spike reflected expanded reporting requirements. Incidents include both liquid spills and gas (vented/flared) releases.

Source: New Mexico OCD, 2022–2025. Deduped by Incident Number.
By Type

Incident Type and Volume, 2025

Liquid spills totaled 1,329 incidents and 5.38 million gallons. Gas releases totaled 37,035 incidents and 9.4 billion cubic feet. Produced water was the most common liquid spilled; natural gas flaring accounted for the vast majority of gas volume.

Causes

Causes of Liquid Spills, 2025

Equipment failure was the leading cause of liquid spills with 651 incidents (49%), followed by corrosion at 309 (23%). Human error accounted for 83 incidents. Together, equipment failure and corrosion represent nearly three-quarters of all liquid spills.

Top 5 causes plus all others. Source: NM OCD, 2025.
Causes

Causes of Gas Releases, 2025

High line pressure was the most common cause of gas releases with 9,003 incidents (24%), followed by equipment failure (8,383) and repair and maintenance (5,736). Midstream emergency maintenance accounted for 4,148 releases. Pipeline quality specifications issues totaled 2,647.

Top 5 causes plus all others. Source: NM OCD, 2025.
Sources

Sources of All Incidents, 2025

Producing wells were the most common incident source with 8,169 reports, followed by separators (5,990) and pipelines (5,008). Tanks of all types accounted for roughly 4,900 combined incidents.

Top 6 spill sources by incident count. Source: NM OCD, 2025.
Operators

Top Liquid Spillers: 2024 vs. 2025 (Gallons)

Devon Energy Production led all operators with 1.02 million gallons of liquid spilled in 2025, up from 343,000 in 2024. EOG Resources ranked second at 460,000 gallons. OXY USA, which led in 2024 with 491,000 gallons, fell to sixth place in 2025 at 272,000.

Top 6 operators by 2025 liquid volume. Source: NM OCD, 2025.
Methane Waste

Top Methane Waste Operators: 2024 vs. 2025 (Billion Cubic Feet)

EOG Resources led all operators in methane waste with 1.38 billion cubic feet in 2025, down slightly from 1.58 billion in 2024. Matador Production ranked second. Civitas Permian and Permian Resources Operating were new entrants to the top five.

Top 5 operators by 2025 methane volume (flared and vented). Source: NM OCD, 2025.
Geography

Liquid Spills by County, 2025

Lea and Eddy counties in the Permian Basin dominated, accounting for nearly all liquid spills. Lea County recorded 605 incidents and 2.84 million gallons. Eddy County had 594 incidents and 2.22 million gallons. San Juan County in the northwest was a distant third.

Geography

Gas Releases by County, 2025

Lea County reported 21,559 gas releases, more than all other counties combined. Eddy County had 14,935. The Permian Basin's two core counties accounted for 98% of all New Mexico gas release incidents.
