Trend

Spills Per Year, 2014–2025

Colorado reported 338 oil and gas spills in 2025, a decrease from 378 in 2024. Spill counts rose sharply from 2016 to 2017 and have remained elevated. The state has recorded 3,892 spills since 2014.

Source: COGCC, 2025. Spill Type: Recent. Deduped by Tracking #.
Volume

Total Volume Spilled Per Year (Gallons), 2021–2025

2022 saw the highest reported volume at 1.46 million gallons. After a sharp decline in 2024, volumes rebounded in 2025, driven largely by a single large spill from QB Energy Operating in the Piceance Basin.

Note: Volume figures are minimums based on operator self-reporting. Many spills list an unknown volume.
Source: COGCC, 2025. Oil, condensate, produced water, E&P waste, flowback fluid, and drilling fluid combined.
Seasonality

Spills by Month, 2025

January was Colorado's peak month with 48 reported spills. Spring (April) saw a secondary high. Summer and fall months were comparatively lower, consistent with prior-year patterns.

Source: COGCC, 2025.
Operators

Spills by Operator, 2025

The top five operators accounted for 45% of all 2025 spills. QB Energy Operating, formed in 2024 to acquire Caerus Oil and Gas assets in the Piceance Basin, led all operators with 46 spills. TEP Rocky Mountain, formerly Terra Energy Partners, saw a 1,400% increase from just one spill in 2024.

Top 5 vs. all others. Source: COGCC, 2025.
Top 10 operators by spill count. Source: COGCC, 2025.
Geography

Spills by County, 2025

Weld County recorded 170 spills (50% of total), reflecting the density of DJ Basin activity. Garfield County in the Piceance Basin ranked second with 61. Spills occurred in 21 of Colorado's 64 counties.

Source: COGCC, 2025. Hover over a county for spill count.
Land Ownership

Spills by Surface Owner, 2025

81% of spills occurred on private land, consistent with the DJ Basin's surface ownership pattern. 10% occurred on federal land managed by the Bureau of Land Management or US Forest Service.

Source: COGCC, 2025.
Spill Size

Spill Size Distribution, 2025

Among the 155 reported oil spills, most fell in the one-to-100-barrel range. Of 256 produced water spills, the five-to-100-barrel range was most common. A substantial share list an unknown volume, reflecting limits in self-reported disclosure.

Oil spills by size (barrels). Source: COGCC, 2025.
Produced water spills by size (barrels). Source: COGCC, 2025.
Proximity

Distance to Sensitive Resources, 2025

Of 338 spills, 100 (30%) reported distance to surface water, 110 (33%) reported distance to water wells, and 77 (23%) reported distance to occupied buildings. Among those reporting, a meaningful share occurred within 500 feet of each resource type.

Note: These figures reflect only spills that reported a proximity measurement. The majority did not disclose distance data.

Year-Over-Year

Volume Spilled by Operator: 2024 vs. 2025 (Gallons)

TEP Rocky Mountain and Laramie Energy both saw large volume increases. Scout Energy and Caerus Piceance saw substantial decreases. QB Energy, new in 2025, reported 445,284 gallons and is not shown here as it had no 2024 baseline.

Source: COGCC, 2025. Volumes in gallons (combined liquids, deduped by Tracking #).
Year-Over-Year

Biggest Volume Changes by Operator, 2024–2025 (Gallons)

TEP Rocky Mountain led all operators in volume increase at +63,084 gallons. Scout Energy Management recorded the largest decrease at -98,448 gallons, having been the second-largest spiller in 2024.
